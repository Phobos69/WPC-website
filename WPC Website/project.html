PROJECT

The Ngonye Project

  • A 180 MW run-of-river hydroelectric power scheme on the Zambezi River in Western Province of Zambia.
  • To generate 855 GWh per year of clean, renewable energy for local demand and export. Equivalent to the power requirements of 750,000 Zambian households.
  • A partnership with the community of the Western Province.
  • A $500m investment with an expected EPC construction cost of US$300m â€“ US$350m, the largest ever private investment in Western Zambia.
  • Financial close in Q4 2019.
  • 36-month construction period.
  • Commercial Operations Date Q1 2023.

Hydrology, Power & Energy

  • A complex hydrological system managed through comprehensive survey, data analysis and modelling.
  • Daily gauge data from 1924 (Livingstone), 1947 (Senanga) and 2007 (Sioma) acquired and processed.
  • Four river gauges installed at the project site operated continuously since 2012.
  • Multiple rounds of bathymetric and Acoustic Doppler Current Profile (ADCP) survey across the project site.
  • Computer hydrodynamic model constructed for scenario testing, environmental impact assessment and energy modelling.
  • Flow in the Zambezi is strongly seasonal with a flood peak in April and low flow from July through to January.
  • Higher flows cause the water level in the gorge downstream of the falls to rise and reduces power generation head.
  • There is a significant variation from year to year in the flow of the river depending on rainfall upstream in the catchment.
  • But increasing head at decreasing flows means that the power output shows considerably less variation from year to year.
  • Coefficient of variation flow: 36%, Coefficient of variation energy: 12%.

Environmental Hydrology

  • Need to safeguard the environmental and social amenity of the river and waterfalls by guaranteeing minimum flows which will not be used for generation (Eflows).
  • Multi-disciplinary Eflow assessment has determined flows in every channel required to maintain aquatic biodiversity, fisheries and the visual aesthetic of the falls.
  • Bathymetric and Lidar topographic surveys completed to inform a computer hydrological model to determine flooding impact and maximum possible head pond elevation.
